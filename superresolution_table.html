Bold values are confirmed directly from the cited paper. Italic values are estimated from context or derived from other reported quantities.

PSF FWHM is the width of the effective point spread function — the resolution of the imaging system. For SMLM rows the PSF is intentionally diffraction-limited (~250 nm) and superresolution arises entirely from the localization precision column.

Localization precision $\sigma$ is the statistical position uncertainty, scaling approximately as FWHM/$\sqrt{N_\mathrm{ph}}$ for an isolated emitter. See the σ scaling write-up for the full derivation.

Total scan time notes. Maurer 2010: 7.2 s/pixel × 50×50 = 5.0 h (confirmed-derivable). Jaskula 2017: 9 s/pixel × ~6400 px est. ≈ 160 h; in practice only small 2D subregions were imaged. STED/GSD/CSD scan times are unreported in the primary papers; estimated values assume ~1–5 ms dwell, a few hundred nm FOV, and Nyquist-sampled steps (2× PSF FWHM), giving seconds to minutes per image. Wildanger 2011 is an exception: each pixel also requires a full ODMR frequency sweep, pushing time/pixel to seconds and total time to hours. SMLM (Pfender, Gu, Barbiero) uses wide-field frame acquisition; total times are estimated at tens of minutes to hours depending on frame count required for sufficient localizations.
